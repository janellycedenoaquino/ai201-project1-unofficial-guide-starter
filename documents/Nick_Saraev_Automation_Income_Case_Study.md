# Nick Saraev: From Medicine Dropout to $100K/Month Automation Empire

Nick Saraev is a Bulgarian-Canadian entrepreneur who pivoted from a path in neuroscience to building a series of automated online businesses. His businesses generate a combined $4M+ per year in revenue. He has never taken outside funding.

---

## Early Income Experiments

Before automation, Nick tested several business models:

**Promotions company (Savage Entertainment):** Nick and friends ran university events and nightclub promotions, making around $30,000 over 18 months. His takeaway: he could touch thousands of lives spending just a few hours per week, and the freedom of controlling his own time was more valuable than the money itself.

**Online courses (Udemy/Skillshare):** Nick and a friend produced around 20 courses over two years, hosting them on Udemy and similar platforms. Investment: ~$2,000 and a few hundred hours. Revenue: over $80,000. He described this as a clear signal that "you could generate outsized results if you wandered off the beaten path."

**Door-to-door marketing agency:** Nick sold local marketing services to brick-and-mortar businesses, eventually scaling with a partner to over $150,000 in revenue and hitting $20,000 in a single month — his first taste of a "real company."

**Wedding videography:** Nick built an SEO funnel and ran paid ads to generate leads. Hit $10,000/month before COVID-19 shut down events overnight, dropping revenue to $0 instantly. This failure directly pushed him toward location-independent software businesses.

---

## 1SecondPainting: First AI Product

After COVID killed his videography business, Nick taught himself programming over five months using self-study resources. He then experimented with AI image generation, fine-tuning a StyleGAN model on abstract art datasets.

He posted the resulting web app on Hacker News on a whim before going to bed. It went viral, hit #1, and generated dozens of requests from labs, journals, and companies the next morning. Because his dev skills weren't strong enough to implement a sign-up system in time, he missed most of the traffic spike.

He eventually added the ability to purchase generated art and peaked at ~$2,200/month, riding the early NFT wave. He sold the site to a private investor the following year.

---

## 1SecondCopy: AI Content Agency to ~$90K/Month

Noticing that GPT-3 was mediocre at code but strong at long-form content, Nick set up freelance profiles on Fiverr and PeoplePerHour and began offering AI-assisted content writing at $0.02/word. Demand came immediately.

He developed scripts to automate article generation from outlines, raised rates progressively, and eventually hired human editors to improve quality. He partnered with Noah, who helped build a staffing model using overseas (Philippines) contractors.

**Revenue progression:**
- ~$3,000/month as a solo freelancer
- Scaled with partner to just under $100,000/month over roughly one year
- Hit the $50,000/month milestone, which had been a lifetime goal

When ChatGPT launched publicly and clients became skeptical of AI-generated content, margins compressed and the business had to scale back. The company continues to operate today in a less automated form.

---

## LeftClick: Automation Consultancy — Productized to $72K/Month Solo

After 1SecondCopy peaked, Nick and Noah started LeftClick, an AI automation consultancy. Initially they pursued complex custom projects, which required hiring skilled contractors — a problem they couldn't solve at scale.

The turning point: a client requested a system similar to one Nick had already built. He duplicated the template, made minor adjustments, and earned ~$1,000 in about 30 minutes. This revealed the real opportunity: **productized automation templates** rather than custom work.

Instead of broad AI automation offerings, Nick broke his services into a small set of high-leverage templates — one-and-done cold email systems, one-click CRMs — and offered only those.

**Result:** Scaled to just over $72,000 in a single month, working solo with occasional virtual assistant help.

The ceiling he hit: the agency model scales with time, whether yours or your team's. Monthly churn, project fires, and the practical limit of ~60 hours/week meant growth was linear at best. He started asking: *what scales further than consulting?*

---

## Make Money With Make: Community to $100K/Month

~6 months into running LeftClick solo, Nick started a YouTube channel focused on Make.com automation. His observation: including "Make.com" in titles drove significantly more views. He built "Make for people who want to make money" as his core content series.

**YouTube growth:**
- Near-zero views for the first two weeks
- ~30 subscribers/day by week two
- ~200 subscribers/day by week four
- ~3,000 subscribers by end of month one
- ~10,000 subscribers by month three

As his audience shifted from people interested in the technology to people wanting to replicate his business model, comments began demanding a private community. He launched Make Money With Make on Skool at $28/month with a 400-member cap.

**What happened:**
- Woke up the next morning making nearly $1,000/month
- Raised prices to slow growth — demand went *up*, not down (perceived scarcity)
- Hit the 400-member cap within ~10 days at $128/month final price
- Was simultaneously making ~$50,000/month from agency work
- Combined income crossed $100,000/month profit in October 2024

His community engagement strategy: spend at least one hour every morning responding to every thread, DMing new members, recording Loom videos to debug technical questions. He credits this unusually high engagement as the primary reason the community grew to the largest on Skool by revenue.

---

## Maker School: Scaled Education Product — $330K/Month

Make Money With Make eventually evolved into Maker School, a $184/month Skool community teaching AI automation agency building using Make.com and n8n.

**Key metrics (as of 2026):**
- 2,200+ paying members at $184/month
- $330,000/month in revenue from Maker School alone
- 10,000+ graduates
- Won the Skool Games competition at ~$290,000 MRR
- Combined businesses: $4M+ annual revenue
- YouTube: 400K+ subscribers, 11.9M total views

The guarantee: your first paying client in 90 days or money back. Nick describes his target student as someone willing to put in high technical effort and consistent client outreach — not a passive income play.

## Key Patterns Across Nick's Career

1. **Each business was a response to a ceiling in the previous one.** Door-to-door → location-dependent. Videography → event-dependent. Content agency → margin-dependent. Consulting → time-dependent. Community → finally scales without trading time directly.

2. **Productization unlocked the first major leverage jump.** Going from custom consulting to duplicatable templates was the difference between $25K/month (stuck) and $72K/month (solo).

3. **Automation was the thread throughout.** From GPT-3 content scripts to Make.com templates to AI customer service — the consistent bet was that automating repeatable tasks would compound faster than hiring people.

4. **Distribution preceded every revenue spike.** Whether it was building a Twitter audience, a YouTube channel, or a Skool community — each income jump was preceded by building an audience that already trusted him before the sale.

5. **Never took outside funding.** Every business self-funded from revenue. Nick describes his $2M annual profit goal as already surpassed; next milestone is $10M.
